Unforgettable Moments In Kabaddi

Bulls’ ‘Hi-Flyer’ Pawan Sehrawat, arguably the best raider of the tournament, became the first player in the Pro Kabaddi League, Season 7, to surpass 100 raid points, albeit in a loss against Puneri Paltans. Sehrawat is an impressive 17 raid points ahead of than next-best Pardeep Narwal, despite having played a game fewer. He is also one of only two raiders in the league averaging over 10 raid points per game, along with Dabang Delhi K.C.’s Naveen Kumar. Sehrawat’s 29-point performance against Bengal Warriors earlier this season was the third-highest individual score in the history of the tournament.

Top Players

Pardeep Narwal (born 16 February 1997) is an Indian kabaddi player who currently plays for the Patna Pirates in VIVO Pro Kabaddi league and the Indian National Kabaddi team. Pardeep is one of the most prominent players in VIVO Pro Kabaddi and is currently the highest raid-point scorer in league history. He is the first to score 900 points in the vivo pkl kabaddi league in season 7. He led the Patna Pirates to three straight VIVO Pro Kabaddi titles between 2016–17 and holds a multitude of the league’s raiding records.

Pawan Kumar Sehrawat is an Indian kabaddi player who currently plays for Bengaluru Bulls in the VIVO Pro Kabaddi league and for Indian Railways in the Kabaddi nationals. His ability to surprise the defenders with his long reach and lower body strength, make him one of the best raiders in the Pro Kabaddi League.

Naveen Kumar made his debut in Season 6 and became the first player born in the 2000s to feature in the league. He finished his first season as Dabang Delhi K.C.'s top-scorer with 177 points and led them to their maiden appearance in the playoffs. Naveen carried the same vein in Season 7, emerging as the league MVP in just his second campaign while taking his team to the final. Blessed with explosive speed and cat-like reflexes, Naveen also scored 22 Super 10s in 23 appearances in Season 7. His ability to deploy his staple move - the 'Running Hand Touch' to score points has made him an offensive juggernaut.

Surender started playing Kabaddi in the left corner for the franchise U Mumba in Season 1 of the Pro Kabaddi League. He represented the Indian National Team in the 2016 Kabaddi World Cup held in Ahmedabad. He, along with his partner, right corner Mohit Chhillar, have since remained the first choice players for the national team in their respective positions. The two have gained a reputation as the most lethal left-right corner combo in the sport of Kabaddi which are the most important positions in the defensive set-up and strategy.

Fazel Atrachali (born 29 March 1992) is an Iranian kabaddi player who currently represents U Mumba in the VIVO Pro Kabaddi League and the Iranian National kabaddi team. Atrachali is one of the most successful foreign players in the history of the VIVO Pro Kabaddi league and is currently the only foreign player to rank among the top 10 tackle points scorer in league history. In the 4th edition of the VIVO Pro Kabaddi league, he won the award of the Best Defender of the Season award. He is the highest-paid foreign player in the pro kabddi league
